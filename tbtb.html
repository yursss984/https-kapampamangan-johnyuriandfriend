Delicious Kapampangan Tibok-Tibok

Background of the Recipe

Tibok-Tibok is a traditional Kapampangan dessert similar to maja blanca, made creamy and rich using carabao's milk. Its name, which translates to "heartbeat," comes from the way the pudding jiggles as it cooks, resembling a heartbeat.
Tibok-Tibok originates from Pampanga, a province celebrated for its culinary artistry. This delicacy highlights the region’s use of carabao milk, a staple ingredient due to its creamy richness. The dish was traditionally prepared during special occasions and fiestas, emphasizing the Kapampangan love for creating indulgent and unique desserts.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups carabao milk (or fresh whole milk as an alternative)
  • 1 cup cornstarch
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)
    FOR TOPPING
      ½ cup toasted coconut curds (latik)

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the carabao milk, cornstarch, sugar, and salt.
  2. Stir well until the cornstarch is fully dissolved and the mixture is smooth.
  3. Pour the milk mixture into a heavy-bottomed saucepan.
  4. Cook over low heat, stirring constantly with a wooden spoon or whisk to prevent lumps.
  5. Continue stirring until the mixture thickens and forms a smooth, glossy consistency. This takes about 10-15 minutes.
  6. Transfer the thickened mixture into a lightly greased rectangular or square dish.
  7. Smooth the surface with a spatula.
  8. Let it cool to room temperature, then refrigerate until fully set (about 2-3 hours).
  9. In a pan, cook coconut cream over low heat until it separates into oil and toasted coconut curds (latik).
  10. Set aside the latik for garnish.
  11. Slice the tibok-tibok into squares or rectangles.
  12. Top each serving with latik and enjoy.
