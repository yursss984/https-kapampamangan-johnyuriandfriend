Delicious Kapampangan Buro

Background of the Recipe

Buro, a Kapampangan delicacy, is a savory and tangy fermented rice dish often paired with grilled or fried fish and vegetables. It is made by fermenting rice with salted fish or shrimp, creating a uniquely flavored accompaniment.
Buro reflects Pampanga’s rich culinary heritage and resourcefulness. Before modern refrigeration, Kapampangans developed fermentation techniques to preserve surplus rice and fish. This method not only prolonged the food’s shelf life but also enhanced its flavor. The sourness of buro mirrors the Filipino fondness for tangy dishes, often paired with salty or savory accompaniments like fried fish to balance the flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably slightly overcooked for better fermentation)
  • ½ cup salted fish (tilapia, dalag, or hito) or shrimp (hipon), cleaned and chopped
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional, to balance the flavor)
  • ½ cup water (adjust as needed)
    For Serving
  • Fried or grilled fish (e.g., tilapia, bangus)
  • Steamed vegetables like eggplant, okra, or bitter gourd (ampalaya)

Instructions

  1. Mash the cooked rice to break it down slightly.
  2. Combine the rice with salted fish or shrimp.
  3. Add salt and sugar (if using). Mix well to distribute the flavors evenly.
  4. Transfer the mixture to a clean glass jar or airtight container.
  5. Pour in water if needed to keep the rice moist, but not too watery.
  6. Cover tightly and let it ferment at room temperature for 3-5 days, depending on the desired sourness.
  7. Stir the mixture daily to prevent spoilage and ensure even fermentation.
    FOR COOKING
  1. Heat oil in a pan and sauté garlic, onions, and tomatoes until fragrant.
  2. Add the fermented rice mixture and cook over low heat for 5-10 minutes until heated through and the smell mellows.
