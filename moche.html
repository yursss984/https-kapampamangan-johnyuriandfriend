Delicious Kapampangan Moche

Background of the Recipe

Sasmuan Delicacies Special Polvoron Mochi is rice balls stuffed with red bean paste and chilled. It comes in a variety of flavors and is highly commercial nowadays, with mochi stores popping up everywhere. Moche is Pampanga’s take on this Oriental snack and the ingredients are basically the same. Glutinous rice stuffed with red bean paste, served with gata or coconut milk. The only difference here aside from the coconut milk is that they aren’t really rolled into balls rather into elongated pockets of bean paste.

Ingredients

    FOR THE DOUGH
  • 1 cup glutinous rice flour (malagkit na harina)
  • ½ cup water
  • 1 tbsp sugar (optional)
    FOR THE FILLING
  • 1 cup sweetened mung bean paste (munggo filling) or ube halaya for variation
    FOR COOKING AND GARNISH
  • 3-4 cups water
  • ½ cup sugar (for the syrup)
  • Toasted sesame seeds (optional)

Instructions

    PREPARE THE DOUGH
  1. In a bowl, mix glutinous rice flour and water until a smooth dough forms.
  2. Knead until pliable and no longer sticky.
    SHAPE THE MOCHE
  1. Take a small piece of dough (about 1 tbsp size) and flatten it into a disk.
  2. Place about ½ tsp of sweetened mung bean paste in the center.
  3. Fold the dough to cover the filling and shape it into a smooth ball.
    COOK THE MOCHE
  1. In a pot, boil water. Drop the prepared balls into the boiling water.
  2. Once the balls float to the surface, let them cook for an additional 2-3 minutes. Then remove with a slotted spoon.
    PREPARE THE SYRUP
  1. In another pot, dissolve sugar in water and bring to a boil.
  2. Add the cooked moche into the syrup and simmer for 1-2 minutes.
    SERVE
  1. Transfer to a serving plate or bowl, drizzle with syrup, and sprinkle with toasted sesame seeds if desired.
