Delicious Kapampangan Biringhe

Background of the Recipe

Biringhe is a traditional Kapampangan dish resembling paella. Made with glutinous rice and a mix of meat, vegetables, and spices, it is richly flavored with coconut milk and turmeric, giving it a vibrant yellow color. It’s often served during special occasions and celebrations.
Biringhe is Pampanga’s version of the Spanish paella, which was introduced to the Philippines during the colonial period. The Kapampangan adaptation reflects the region's local ingredients and cooking methods, replacing saffron with turmeric for its yellow color and earthy flavor. The use of glutinous rice and coconut milk emphasizes the Filipino palate's preference for rich, creamy dishes.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups glutinous rice (malagkit)
  • 1 cup regular rice
  • 3 cups coconut milk
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder (or fresh turmeric, grated)
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger, julienned
  • 1 medium carrot, diced
  • 1 cup cooked chicken, shredded
  • ½ cup chorizo, sliced
  • ½ cup shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • ½ cup green peas
  • ½ cup raisins (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce or soy sauce (to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil

Instructions

  1. Rinse the glutinous and regular rice together and soak in water for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat.
  3. Sauté garlic, onion, and ginger until fragrant.
  4. Add the chicken, chorizo, and shrimp, and cook until lightly browned.
  5. Stir in carrots, green peas, and raisins (if using).
  6. Season with fish sauce or soy sauce and turmeric powder, mixing well to coat everything evenly.
  7. Add the soaked rice to the pan and mix well with the sautéed ingredients.
  8. Pour in the coconut milk and chicken broth. Stir to combine.
  9. Cover the pan and let the rice cook over low heat, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking. Add water if needed to fully cook the rice.
  10. Cook until the rice is tender and the liquid is fully absorbed.
  11. Adjust seasoning as needed.
  12. Garnish with slices of boiled egg or additional shrimp, if desired.
  13. Serve warm.
