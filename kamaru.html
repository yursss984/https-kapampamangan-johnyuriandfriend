Delicious Kapampangan Adobong Kamaru

Background of the Recipe

Adobong Kamaru is a unique and exotic Kapampangan delicacy made from mole crickets (kamaru) cooked in the classic adobo style. It offers a savory, tangy, and slightly nutty flavor, showcasing Pampanga’s adventurous culinary traditions.
Adobong Kamaru is a testament to the resourcefulness and boldness of Kapampangan cuisine. Crickets, which thrive in rice fields, have been a sustainable food source in Pampanga for generations. Their inclusion in local dishes highlights the Kapampangans' willingness to explore nontraditional ingredients.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups kamaru (mole crickets), cleaned and washed
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 piece bay leaf
  • Optional: chili peppers for a spicy kick

Instructions

  1. Wash the kamaru thoroughly in water to remove dirt and debris. Drain and pat dry.
  2. Optional: Blanch the crickets in boiling water for 2 minutes to reduce any earthy taste, then drain.
  3. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Sauté garlic and onion until fragrant and lightly golden.
  4. Add the cleaned kamaru to the pan and stir-fry for 3-5 minutes until they begin to crisp up.
  5. Pour in vinegar and soy sauce, and let it simmer without stirring for 1-2 minutes to cook off the acidity.
  6. Add water, bay leaf, and ground black pepper. Mix well.
  7. Lower the heat and let the mixture simmer until the sauce thickens and coats the crickets, about 10-15 minutes.
  8. For a spicier version, add chili peppers during the simmering process.
  9. Transfer to a serving dish and enjoy as a unique appetizer or side dish with steamed rice.
